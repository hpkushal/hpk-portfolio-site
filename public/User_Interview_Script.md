# User Interview Script Guide

## 1. Pre-Interview Checklist
*   [ ] Confirm consent for recording (video + audio)
*   [ ] Review user profile/background (CRM, previous interactions)
*   [ ] Test recording equipment and backup (Zoom, Loom, or dedicated tool)
*   [ ] Prepare note-taking tool (Notion, Dovetail, or AI transcription)
*   [ ] Set up AI transcription (Otter.ai, Grain, or built-in)
*   [ ] Have prototype/mockups ready if needed
*   [ ] Prepare incentive confirmation (if applicable)

---

## 2. Introduction (5 mins)
*Build rapport and set expectations*

*   "Hi [Name], thanks for joining me today. I really appreciate your time."
*   "I'm [Your Name], a Product Manager at [Company]."
*   "The goal of this chat is to learn about your experience with [Topic]. There are no right or wrong answers—I'm here to learn from you."
*   "This will take about [30-45] minutes."
*   "Is it okay if I record this for internal note-taking? The recording is only for our team and won't be shared externally."
*   "Do you have any questions before we start?"

---

## 3. Warm-Up / Context (5 mins)
*Understand their world before diving deep*

*   "Tell me a bit about your role and what you do day-to-day."
*   "Walk me through a typical day when you use [Product/Service]."
*   "What tools or processes do you rely on most?"
*   "How has AI or automation changed how you work?" *(If relevant)*

---

## 4. Deep Dive / Core Questions (20-30 mins)

### Understanding the Problem
*   "What is the hardest part about [Task/Workflow]?"
*   "Can you tell me about the last time you encountered this problem?"
*   "Why was that hard?" *(Ask 'Why' up to 5 times to get to root cause)*
*   "How often does this happen?"
*   "What's the impact when this goes wrong?"

### Evaluating Current Solutions
*   "How do you currently solve this problem?"
*   "What do you like about that approach? What frustrates you?"
*   "Have you tried any other solutions? What happened?"
*   "Are you using any AI tools for this today? How's that going?"

### Exploring the Ideal State
*   "If you had a magic wand, how would you fix this?"
*   "What would 'good' look like for you?"
*   "How would you know if a solution was working?"

### Priorities & Trade-offs
*   "If you could only fix one thing, what would it be?"
*   "What would you be willing to give up to get [desired outcome]?"
*   "How much time/money would solving this be worth to you?"

---

## 5. Feature/Concept Feedback (Optional - 10 mins)
*If testing prototypes or concepts*

*   "I'm going to show you a concept. Please think aloud as you look at it—I want to hear your raw reactions."
*   "What is your first impression?"
*   "What do you think this does?"
*   "What would you expect to happen if you clicked [element]?"
*   "What's confusing or unclear?"
*   "What's missing that you would need?"
*   "On a scale of 1-10, how useful would this be? Why?"

**For AI-powered features specifically:**
*   "Would you trust this AI-generated [output]? Why or why not?"
*   "What would make you trust it more?"
*   "How would you want to correct or adjust the AI's suggestions?"

---

## 6. Wrap-Up (5 mins)
*Leave on a positive note and open the door for future contact*

*   "Is there anything else you think I should know that we didn't cover?"
*   "What's the one thing you most want us to improve?"
*   "Can we contact you again for follow-up questions or to test new features?"
*   "Would you be interested in being part of our beta/early access program?"
*   "Thanks so much for your time—this was incredibly helpful!"

---

## 7. Post-Interview Actions
*   [ ] Write up key insights within 24 hours (while fresh)
*   [ ] Use AI to generate initial summary, then refine
*   [ ] Tag themes and quotes in research repository (Dovetail, Notion)
*   [ ] Share relevant clips with team
*   [ ] Send thank-you note to participant
*   [ ] Update user profile with new insights
*   [ ] Add to synthesis document for pattern analysis

## Interview Best Practices

### Do's ✅
- Ask open-ended questions
- Let silence work (people will fill it)
- Follow interesting threads, even if off-script
- Take verbatim quotes
- Note body language and emotions
- Ask for specific examples and stories

### Don'ts ❌
- Lead the witness ("Don't you think X is better?")
- Talk more than 30% of the time
- Defend your product or ideas
- Ask yes/no questions
- Rush through the script
- Promise features or timelines
